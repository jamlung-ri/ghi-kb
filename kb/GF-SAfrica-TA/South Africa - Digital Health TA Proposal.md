# **Overview** 

The Regenstrief Institute and its South African-based collaborators are delighted to have the opportunity to partner with the Global Fund to provide catalytic technical assistance to the National Department of Health (NDOH) of South Africa. We have first-hand experience with the countries’ efforts to further strengthen the effectiveness of their digital health investments and understand their strong focus to further develop the capacity of the digital health team within the agency. In our previous interactions with the team’s leadership, we’ve learned of their desire to work alongside neutral, unbiased, deep subject matter experts in further developing practical, forward-looking, and politically viable digital health strategies for the country. If awarded this contract, we will leverage these resources to offer peer mentorship to the NDOH Digital Health team and support a few key goals:

* Operationalize the outputs from the upcoming NDOH Digital Health Convergence Workshop through co-authorship support on the new National Digital Health Strategy and Interoperability Framework  
* Offering regular, ongoing adaptive technical assistance based upon the needs of the NDOH team including both digital health subject matter expertise and COBIT training,   
* Serving as the digital health team’s private, neutral sounding board for strategic planning and operational support  
* Sponsoring Africa-based travel for targeted digital health conferences and workshop

# **Conceptual Approach and Methodology** 

Our planned approach aligns with the National Department of Health’s (NDOH) vision of "better health for all South Africans enabled by digital health" by combining global best practices with deeply contextualized, locally-led execution. As the Regenstrief Global Health Informatics (GHI) team, we bring over two decades of experience as a founding leader of the OpenHIE community, and one of the first global health informatics programs in the world. We view this engagement as less like a consultancy and more as an opportunity to further enhance our relationship with the South African NDOH. Durable success of this effort should be designed as a partnership for sustainability, empowering the NDOH to own, evolve, and govern its digital ecosystem long after our engagement with the team might end.

Our methodology is built on four strategic pillars:

**1\. Collaborative Strategy Co-Creation (The "Alignment" Pillar)**

* **Context:** We recognize that the **2025–2030 National Digital Health Strategy** must be more than a published document; it must be a viable, pragmatic roadmap for implementation.  
* **Approach:** We will not "write for" the NDOH but "write with" them. Leveraging outcomes from the **Digital Health Convergence Workshop**, we will facilitate high-touch engagement sessions to ensure the new strategy and 5-Year Roadmap reflect the realities of South Africa’s diverse health landscape.  
* **Tooling:** We will utilize the **World Bank Digital Health Blueprint Toolkit** and **ITU/WHO National eHealth Strategy Toolkit** to ensure alignment with global standards while customizing for local priorities like NHI (National Health Insurance).

**2\. Architecting for Interoperability (The "Technical" Pillar)**

* **Context:** Fragmented systems hinder the "one patient, one record" vision. The **Digital Health Architecture Blueprint** requires a robust, standards-based foundation.  
* **Approach:** As primary architects of **OpenHIE**, we will bring deep subject matter expertise in **FHIR**, **IHE profiles**, and **Global Goods[^1]** (e.g., Open Concept Lab (OCL), OpenMRS, OpenHIM etc). We will support the NDOH in defining an **Interoperability Framework** that is pragmatic, prioritizing high-impact use cases (e.g., lab exchange, patient identity) that deliver immediate value while building towards a mature, integrated ecosystem.  
* **Output:** A "living" Architecture Blueprint that serves as a practical guide for software implementation and integration, not just a theoretical model.

**3\. Capacity Building as a Legacy (The "People" Pillar)**

* **Context:** Sustainable digital transformation requires a skilled workforce. The TOR emphasizes COBIT 2019 training to strengthen governance.  
* **Approach:** We will move beyond generic certification by **contextualizing COBIT 2019** for the public health sector. Our training will blend theoretical frameworks with real-world scenarios relevant to the NDOH’s environment (e.g., governing data privacy in a federated health system).  
* **Mentorship:** Beyond formal training, we offer "peer-to-peer" technical mentorship, connecting NDOH staff with our senior architects and team of experts to troubleshoot live challenges during the project period and beyond.

**4\. Implementation Science & Evidence Generation (The "Impact" Pillar)**

* **Context:** The Global Fund’s **Digital Health Impact Accelerator (DHIA)** prioritizes measurable impact.  
* **Approach:** Drawing on Regenstrief’s research heritage, we will embed implementation science principles into the project. This means establishing clear Success Metrics early (e.g., maturity level improvements, transaction volume growth) and using them to drive decision-making. We will assist the NDOH in setting up a lightweight Monitoring & Evaluation (M\&E) framework that tracks not just technical uptime, but actual health system strengthening outcomes.

In short, we offer the NDOH a partner that "speaks the language" of global standards but "walks the walk" of local implementation. By leveraging our role as a steward of global public goods, bringing additional academic rigor, we ensure the solutions designed are easy to consume, technically sound, globally compliant, and, most importantly, owned and sustainable by South Africa.

Operationally, we’ve chosen to partner with South African collaborators to enable strategic engagement and minimal travel. We will partner with collaborators with whom we have had a strong working relationship in support of the Rwanda HIE work and OpenHIE, and are known to the NDOH. Within the larger Regenstrief Institute, there are multiple deep subject matter experts in areas such as identity management, terminology management, and health information standardization. We will draw upon the entire organization, based upon the specific NDOH needs. Finally, as a steward of multiple communities of practice, we will attempt to bring volunteers from those communities as relevant in further support of the longer term work.

# **Project Timeline & Milestones**

**Phase 1: Inception and Alignment (Month 1: February)**

* Mobilize the team, hold a virtual kickoff session, and establish working groups with the South Africa NDOH Digital Health Team.  
* Attend the **Digital Health Convergence Workshop** (anticipated Feb 2026\) to gather and learn requirements to align with national priorities.  
* Review and begin edits of existing materials (Strategy 2019-2024, Draft 2025-2030 Strategy) to inform the work plan.

  **Key Milestone \-** Inception Report Submitted**:** Details the final task list, deliverables, and schedule (\~5 pages, due end of Month 1).

**Phase 2: Strategy and Architecture Finalization (Months 2–4: March – May)**

* Consolidate Convergence Workshop outcomes into structured inputs for the strategy updates.  
* Draft and finalize the update to the **National Digital Health Strategy (2025–2030)**.  
* Draft and finalize the **Digital Health Architecture Blueprint / Interoperability Framework** and **5-year Roadmap**.

  **Key Milestone \-** Strategy & Architecture documents approved**:** Formal sign-off by NDOH Digital Health Lead and Global Fund on the updated Strategy, Blueprint, Framework and Roadmap.

**Phase 3: Capacity Building Coordination (Months 3–4: April – May)**

* Launch the **COBIT 2019 Training Program** for \~20 NDOH staff, covering all three proposed tracks.  
* Execute training via online instructor-led sessions (spread over 4–6 weeks, max 2–3 hours/session) to accommodate busy schedules.  
* Tailor training content to include specific digital health, interoperability, and data governance contexts.

  **Key Milestone \-** COBIT Certifications Completion**:** Successful training and certification of identified NDOH staff.

**Phase 4: Implementation Support (Months 2–5: March – June)**

* Provide ongoing technical support to the Digital Health Unit to troubleshoot challenges and answer technical queries.  
* Track progress on the "Post-Workshop Action Plan" generated from the Convergence Workshop.

  **Key Milestone \-** Progress Reports Submitted**:** Two reports (3-4 pages each) detailing action plan status, stakeholder engagements, and risk mitigation.

**Phase 5: Knowledge Sharing and Sustainability (Months 1–6: Ongoing)**

* Coordinate logistics and sponsorship for \~6 person-trips to regional digital health conferences/workshops to foster experience sharing.  
* Facilitate knowledge transfer to ensure the momentum of implementation continues beyond the consultancy.

  **Key Milestone \-** Final Project Closeout**:** Completion of all capacity-building support and final acceptance of all deliverables by NDOH and Global Fund.

# **Regenstrief Previous Work History** 

Regenstrief has considerable real-world experience in the development, use, and maintenance of health information system standards, as key contributors to HL7, FHIR, and LOINC. Regenstrief’s Global Health Informatics (GHI) program has direct working experience in many African countries, as we’ve previously worked to develop HIE strategic roadmaps in Rwanda, Nigeria, Tanzania, Kenya, Malawi, and Ethiopia, while offering executive coaching and behind-the-scenes support to their digital health leadership. 

Our recent Global Fund-supported work highlights our ability to deliver high-impact results:

* **Ethiopia (2023-2024)**: We conducted a rapid costing of Ethiopia’s 2021 Digital Health Blueprint, transforming a high-level vision into a 7-year cost roadmap. This involved intensive collaboration with the Ministry of Health to define investment needs for infrastructure, data systems, and human resources.


Examples of other related projects include: 

* **OpenHIE and OpenHIE Academy 2015 \- 2025:** We are a founding OpenHIE organization, and the active secretariat of the OpenHIE (OHIE) community. As a part of our work, we provide strategic architecture leadership and host the OpenHIE academy and lead sub communities such as the terminology services community. Many countries (e.g., [Malawi](https://wiki.ohie.org/spaces/CP/pages/338329608/Malawi), [Sri Lanka](https://wiki.ohie.org/spaces/CP/pages/154796044/Sri+Lanka), [Rwanda](https://wiki.ohie.org/spaces/CP/pages/154796053/Rwanda), [Lao](https://wiki.ohie.org/spaces/CP/pages/343146535/Lao+People+s+Democratic+Republic)) reflect the OpenHIE digital health public infrastructure and standards approach in their strategy.   
* **LOINC®:** (Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes) is a universal coding system for identifying laboratory tests, clinical observations, and health measurements, providing a common language for electronic health records (EHRs) and data exchange. Regenstrief is the LOINC creator and maintainer of LOINC and fosters partnerships with other organizations such as SNOMED, HL7 and WHO (ICD-11) to promote and support the use of standard terminologies.   
* **Digital Innovation for Pandemic Control (GIZ DIPC) 2022 \- 2025:** RI researched training gaps and training needed to sustain implementation of global goods and HIS interventions, developed learner personas, created a peer learning community, and developed learning materials for capacity strengthening, available online as a global good.   
* **CDC TAP Identity Management \- Nigeria 2024 \- 2025:** Regenstrief worked with the Nigerian MoH and their implementing partner PHIS3 to review their PIM implementation strategy, to support scaling and strengthen security strategies. The team partnered with PHIS3 to educate key stakeholders, propose and socialize solutions, analyze data in a secure environment, identify opportunities for improvement, and recommended next steps.
