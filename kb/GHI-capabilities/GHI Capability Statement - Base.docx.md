# Global Health Informatics Capability Statement

The Regenstrief Institute Global Health Informatics program (GHI) is a growing cadre of researchers, subject-matter experts, and technology practitioners committed to improving global health equity by encouraging standards-based, architected, and artificial intelligence approaches to health information technology (HIT) and enabling data-driven healthcare. We come from diverse backgrounds and cultures, and our areas of health informatics subject-matter expertise include:

* Healthcare delivery and clinical workflow analysis  
* Client identity management  
* Health / clinical terminologies  
* Public health and surveillance information systems  
* Electronic medical records  
* Business analysis and change management  
* Terminology (semantic interoperability) and term mapping   
* Training/capacity strengthening

Our strategy for change is centered on the belief that we can affect sustainable improvement in the global health community by contributing directly to “trailblazer” HIT implementation projects, helping develop HIT communities of practice, and partnering with MOHs to build informatics capacity and solutions. We work alongside worldwide colleagues to define and implement strategies, approaches, and open technologies designed for use in resource-limited settings.

GHI collaborates with other organizations and countries by combining a people-centered approach with deep HIT subject matter expertise and project management support that is technical and strategic, yet grounded in practical experiences working in solidarity within resource-constrained environments.

## Regenstrief GHI Team Key Capabilities

### Strategic Advising

* eHealth strategy, investment roadmaps, and/or implementation plan development  
* eHealth governance structure and design   
* eHealth policy development  
* Operationalizing eHealth strategies within constraints  
* Design of infrastructure to measure/monitor health outcome improvement  
* Integration of HIT as an effector for health improvement  
* Health systems research and design   
* Information exchange standards and strategy

### Capacity Strengthening

* Document and synthesize capacity-strengthening needs   
* Co-creation of capacity-strengthening plans and roadmaps  
* Conducting workshops or training sessions tailored to your context   
* Identifying and creating learning resources  
* Framework and model development for understanding complex topics and applying theory to practice  
* Training and consulting for specific eHealth architecture components, including setup and operationalization, standards adoption, governance, etc., for terminology service, client registry, and electronic medical records

### Community Building 

* Strategic, tactical, and operational guidance  
* Community leadership/management   
* Community of practice formation, design, and leadership  
* Virtual and in-person workshop planning and facilitation  
* Community promotion and engagement for knowledge sharing

### Technical Assistance

* HIT application evaluation, design, development, implementation planning, and/or oversight  
* Interoperability and health information exchange architecture, design and implementation  
* Terminology service integration within health information exchanges, including governance framework design and operationalization  
* Recognition and development of processes to support the use of health information syntactic and semantic standards  
* Virtual and in-person people-centered workshop planning and facilitation

### Terminology Management & Standards Development

* Terminology service architecture, deployment, and operational management  
* Standards-based guideline development (e.g., WHO SMART Guidelines and FHIR Implementation Guides)  
* Mapping terms to FHIR resources to create computable guidelines  
* Country advisory services for national terminology strategy and adoption  
* Training and capacity building for terminology standards implementation  
* Development and maintenance of AI-supported terminology mapping tools

## Mission Vision Values

### Our Mission

To achieve health equity for resource-constrained communities through health information technologies.

### Our Vision

A world where health information technology directly contributes to the sustained elimination of global health disparities.

### Values

#### Community

We engage in global collaboration with people with diverse talents and backgrounds who are solving problems in healthcare.

#### Openness

Transparency and sharing are our guiding principles as we make our knowledge, resources, programs, and tools available to all.

#### Humility

Those we serve are our teachers.

#### Sustainability

Our stakeholders build local capacity as they develop, implement, and maintain effective health information systems.

#### Empowerment

We work with global innovators to eliminate health inequities and support underserved communities.
